Work
After a small discussion on slashdot I realised that if I were to take up contracting and work 1-2 days a week on average, I could happily maintain my current lifestyle (although I won't really be saving any money). This seems incredibly attractive, as a 5-day weekend every week would give me lots of time to do more fulfilling things than writing code for someone else. I'll need to sit down and figure out all the pros and cons of this scenario.

House

Jul 7 , 2000
House
We made an offer on the house, and someone else made an offer for the full asking price, so they got it. I'm not too upset, as there are plenty of other nicer houses out there, and in a few months time I'll be in a much better position to buy. What I find interesting is that both Ian and Sofie, and Benni and Tara are looking at buying houses now. It's almost looking like a Monopoly game.

Banks
One of the banks who I was talking about home-loans with wants to give me a complimentary credit card with an outrageously large credit limit. I'll be handing it back to them.

Diplomacy
Junwei is winning, and after this game I am going to have to take lessons from him about how he does this. Italy (Phil) is eating out of Junwei's hand, and is probably going to spur Junwei on to victory. I think we're going to have a hard time convincing Phil that possibly losing is better than helping Junwei and definitely losing. Unfortunately being the second largest power, everything thinks I'm trying to nobble Junwei so that I can win. The truth is I want to nobble Junwei so that I don't lose. I'm much rather force Junwei to draw than have him win the game. I'm researching stalemate lines to see what I can do.

Work
Negotiating with Dan to try and get a nine day fortnight. Believe it or not, I actually want more of a life. There's been a lot of proceedural stuff flying around recently, and not all of it is stable yet. It should be good once it is, because as bug-boy I want quality more than most.

A legacy version of one of our products is now finally finished, the customers using it having upgraded to the latest and greatest. This makes me very happy.

House

Jul 1 , 2000
House
Made an offer on the house today, and there seems to be a very good possibility that we'll be accepted as well. We should know in the next couple of days what's happening. If everything goes well we'll be moving in about three months time.

Permaculture
The local trainstation had a big impressive permaculture poster up which appears to have been sponsored by the train company. Surprisingly enough it wasn't just "trains are good" propaganda, but made mention of low-impact housing, backyard food systems, and (surprisingly enough) Critical Mass.. It's good to see things like this going up around the city -- sometimes it seems that any poster advocating sustainability lives inside a University campus, or leads a very brief existence before it's plastered over with concert posters.

Work
Friday drinks are finally becoming a little more organised and are running more smoothly. There was a small problem where we miscalculated the horde of brew we were purchasing, and a couple of the pizzas we ordered didn't come through. Depsite this everything seems to have gone well, and we have what I hope to be a sustainable system in place.

I'm expecting to see a few sparks on Monday regarding some mis-communication problems, and I think that we really need a good priority system implemented for tasks, as not everyone sees eye-to-eye about which tasks are the most important.

Hacky Injury

Jun 26 , 2000
Hacky Injury
An important lesson for all those people planning to play hackey-sack in an office environment. If you're playing without shoes, and the hackey comes down just on the corner of a table, pull your kick. Table-corners hurt a lot when you kick them with the upper bits of your feet.

Rockclimbing
Had a great time rockclimbing. Kathryn came with us this week, and showed an excellent amount of skill for a first-time climber. I climbed the black that I'd been working on for a couple of weeks, and decided that it's either been made a lot easier, or I've become a much better climber. At the end of the day we had races up the red climb on the training wall, and I lost. I blame it on my colour-blindness, having to always look twice before going for a hold.

Houses
After visiting the real-estate agent we decided to put our offer in on Saturday when we'd be eligible for the $7000 government bonus for first home-buyers.

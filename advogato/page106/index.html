Diplomacy
Junwei is winning, and after this game I am going to have to take lessons from him about how he does this. Italy (Phil) is eating out of Junwei's hand, and is probably going to spur Junwei on to victory. I think we're going to have a hard time convincing Phil that possibly losing is better than helping Junwei and definitely losing. Unfortunately being the second largest power, everything thinks I'm trying to nobble Junwei so that I can win. The truth is I want to nobble Junwei so that I don't lose. I'm much rather force Junwei to draw than have him win the game. I'm researching stalemate lines to see what I can do.

Permaculture
The local trainstation had a big impressive permaculture poster up which appears to have been sponsored by the train company. Surprisingly enough it wasn't just "trains are good" propaganda, but made mention of low-impact housing, backyard food systems, and (surprisingly enough) Critical Mass.. Hacky Injury
An important lesson for all those people planning to play hackey-sack in an office environment. If you're playing without shoes, and the hackey comes down just on the corner of a table, pull your kick. Table-corners hurt a lot when you kick them with the upper bits of your feet.

Finance::Quote

Jun 24 , 2000
Finance::Quote
Awright! Finance::Quote now does automatic currency conversion, and makes it dead-simple to use:

use Finance::Quote;
my $quoter = Finance::Quote->new;
$quoter->set_currency("AUD");
my %info = $quoter->fetch("nasdaq","LNUX");

%info will now contain information about VA Linux Systems, except all information will be in Australian dollars, rather than the US dollars which Yahoo supplies it in. This makes me very happy indeed, and means that it's now very very very easy to write stock tickers and the like which show information in your local currency.
